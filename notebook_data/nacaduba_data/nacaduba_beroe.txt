Nacaduba beroe neon (The Opaque Six-line Blue) is a member of the berenice group (the 6-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing possesses a pair of pale subbasal lines across the cell.

Primary Diagnostic Feature: It is characterized by having the area between the postdiscal striae unicolorous and of the ground color.

Distinguishing Feature (Male): The wing bases on the underside are darkened, which helps distinguish it from the similar N. kurava. Additionally, the underside markings are not visible by transparency on the upperside.

Distinguishing Feature (Female): The upperside of the female features a lustrous purple patch on the forewing.

General Pattern: The markings are formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae arranged in the usual Lycaenine pattern.

Size Comparison: It is generally larger than N. berenice.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: Typically ranges between 12.0 mm and 15.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
