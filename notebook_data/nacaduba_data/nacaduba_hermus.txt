Nacaduba hermus swatipa (The Pale 4-line Blue) belongs to the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Marking Arrangement: On the hindwing underside, the inner submarginal and outer postdiscal striae in spaces 4 and 5 are nearly touching.

Striae Color: The striae are pale buff in males and whitish in females.

Tornal Markings: The black tornal spot in space 2 on the hindwing underside is crowned by a brownish-orange ring.

Appearance: Males have a frosted appearance on the upperside due to an abundance of white ribbon scales, which distinguishes them from similar species like N. subperusia.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: Typically ranges between 13.0 mm and 15.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
