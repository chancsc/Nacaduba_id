Nacaduba russelli belongs to the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba. It is a very rare species.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: On the underside of the forewing, it lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Postdiscal Band: The postdiscal band consists of a double series of pale striae that are inwardly bounded by darker lines. The space between these striae is typically paler than the ground color.

General Pattern: The underside markings are formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae arranged in the usual Lycaenine pattern. 

Comparison: It resembles Nacaduba pavana on the underside but is distinguished by being more richly colored on the upperside in both sexes.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: Typically measures between 11.0 mm and 12.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
