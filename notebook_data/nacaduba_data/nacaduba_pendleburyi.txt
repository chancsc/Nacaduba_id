Nacaduba pendleburyi pendleburyi (The Malayan 4-line Blue) is a member of the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) within the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Primary Diagnostic Feature: This species is easily recognized by having a forewing black border that expands appreciably toward the apex on the upperside, which is a character reflected in the identification of its subspecies as well.

Marking Definition: The markings on the underside are formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae. 

Striae Arrangement: It is characterized by having the inner submarginal and outer postdiscal striae on the hindwing underside separated by at least half the width of the postdiscal band.

Ribbon Scales: Males of this species lack white ribbon scales on the upperside, which helps distinguish it from similar species like N. hermus.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: The forewing typically measures between 14.0 mm and 16.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: Like other members of its group in Malaysia, this species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
