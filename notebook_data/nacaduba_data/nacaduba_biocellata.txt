Nacaduba biocellata, also known as the Two-spotted Line Blue, is a member of the berenice group (the 6-line Blues) within the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: As part of the 6-line Blues, the underside of the forewing possesses a pair of pale subbasal lines across the cell.

Primary Diagnostic Feature: This species is uniquely characterized by having two equal-sized, orange-ringed black spots on the underside of the hindwing.

Transparency: These prominent black spots on the underside are visible by transparency on the wing's upperside.

Tail Information

Tail: Like all other members of the genus Nacaduba in the region, this species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
