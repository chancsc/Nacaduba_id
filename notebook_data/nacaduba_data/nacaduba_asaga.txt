Nacaduba asaga is a rare species belonging to the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba. It is recognized as a Bornean species and has not been recorded on the Malay Peninsula.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: As a member of the 4-line Blues, it lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the forewing cell on the underside.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal band is curved in towards the base and is often slightly dislocated at vein 6.

Hindwing Markings: The inner submarginal and outer postdiscal striae are well separated, specifically by at least half the width of the postdiscal band.

Striae Appearance: The underside markings consist of narrow, well-defined striae that are white or whitish in color.

Ground Color: The area between the postdiscal striae is unicolorous and of the ground color.

Upperside and Size

Upperside (Male): The wings are purple with a distinct steely grey tint. The black border is described as a "thread" throughout.

Ribbon Scales: Males of this species lack white ribbon scales on the upperside.

Wing Length: The forewing length measures between 15.0 mm and 16.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
