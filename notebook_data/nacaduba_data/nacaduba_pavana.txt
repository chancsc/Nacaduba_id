Nacaduba pavana belongs to the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba. It is represented in Malaysia by two subspecies: vajuva (Langkawi) and singapura (Singapore and the mainland).

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Marking Definition: The postdiscal band is composed of a double series of pale striae that are inwardly bounded by darker lines. The space between these striae is typically paler than the ground color.

Distinguishing Feature: It is characterized by its relatively small size and a specific upperside color in males (pale greyish blue) which helps distinguish it from similar species like N. russelli.

Ground Color: The underside is typically a buff-grey with the characteristic Nacaduba pattern of short, dull whitish striae.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: Typically ranges between 11.0 mm and 12.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
