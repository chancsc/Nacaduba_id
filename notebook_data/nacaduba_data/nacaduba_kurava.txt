Nacaduba kurava nemana (The Transparent Six-line Blue) is a member of the berenice group (the 6-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing possesses a pair of pale subbasal lines across the cell.

Postdiscal Markings: The postdiscal striae are inwardly bounded by darker lines, and the space between these striae is paler than the ground color.

Distinguishing Feature (Male): The underside markings are visible by transparency on the upperside of the male.

Distinguishing Feature (Female): The upperside of the female is pale shining blue, turning whitish beyond the forewing cell.

Comparison with N. beroe: Unlike N. beroe, the wing bases on the underside are not darkened.

General Pattern: The markings are formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae arranged in the usual Lycaenine pattern.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: Typically ranges between 13.5 mm and 15.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
