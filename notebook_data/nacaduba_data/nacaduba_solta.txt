Nacaduba solta is a rare species belonging to the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Primary Diagnostic Feature: The postdiscal band on the underside of the forewing is continuous and almost straight from vein 3 to vein 7.

Marking Definition: The underside markings are formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae.

Upperside (Male) Distinction: The upperside of the male is purple with a slight bronzy tint, and the black border is a thread throughout. Males of this species lack white ribbon scales.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: The forewing typically measures between 15.0 mm and 16.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
