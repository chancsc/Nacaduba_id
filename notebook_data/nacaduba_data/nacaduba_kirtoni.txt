Nacaduba kirtoni is a member of the berenice group (the 6-line Blues) and is known only from high elevations above 1200 m.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: As a member of the 6-line Blues, the underside of the forewing possesses a pair of pale subbasal lines across the cell.

Primary Diagnostic Feature: The underside is nearly identical to that of Nacaduba berenice, making it extremely difficult to separate them without examination of the male genitalia.

Ground Color: Typically a buff-grey.

Pattern: The markings consist of pairs of whitish striae arranged in the usual Lycaenine pattern, with marginal markings of the same color.

Tornal Markings: On the hindwing underside, space 2 features a black marginal spot dusted with metallic green scales and crowned by a pale orange halo.

Distinguishing Features:
Unlike Nacaduba beroe, the wing bases on the underside are not darkened.

The underside markings are barely visible by transparency on the upperside of the male.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
