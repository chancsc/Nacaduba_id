Nacaduba angusta kerriana (The White-edged Blue) belongs to the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Primary Diagnostic Feature: The underside of the hindwing features a prominent, rounded black submarginal spot in space 6.

Marking Definition: It is characterized by particularly large black submarginal spots in both spaces 2 and 6 on the hindwing.

Striae: The underside stripes (striae) are broader and more diffuse than in many other species within the genus.

Ground Color: Typically buff-grey with markings formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: The forewing typically measures between 14.0 mm and 15.5 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
