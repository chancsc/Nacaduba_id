Nacaduba subperusia belongs to the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba. It occurs in Malaysia as two subspecies: lysa (lowlands) and intricata (highlands).

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Striae Arrangement: On the hindwing underside, the inner submarginal and outer postdiscal striae in spaces 4 and 5 are nearly touching.

Tornal Markings: The black tornal spot in space 2 on the hindwing underside is crowned by a clear orange ring. This helps distinguish it from the very similar N. hermus, which has a brownish-orange ring.

Ground Color and Striae: The markings consist of pairs of short striae.
In subspecies lysa, the striae are whitish.

In subspecies intricata, the striae are pale buff.

Subspecies Variation

Nacaduba subperusia lysa: Found at low to moderate elevations. It is generally smaller, with a forewing length of 13.0 mm to 15.0 mm.

Nacaduba subperusia intricata: Found at high elevations (above 1100 m). It is larger (15.0 mm to 16.0 mm) and duller, with the veins on the upperside appearing darker and more contrasting.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
