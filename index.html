Nacaduba ID

An interactive identification App for Malaysian Nacaduba butterflies, using underside markings and ID keys.

Introduction

Nacaduba ID helps you put a name to a Malaysian Six-line Blue (Nacaduba) butterfly from a photo of its wing underside. Rather than memorising dozens of similar-looking species, you work through a small set of clearly illustrated characters — tail shape, spot position, banding pattern — and the app narrows the candidates down for you.

What each section is for

Feature Scoring

Mark the characters you can clearly see in your photo, in any order. Every candidate species is scored and ranked live as you go, and any feature you leave blank never counts against a match.

Species Search

Browse or search the full list of taxa covered by this key. Each species page shows its identification characters, reference photos, and the route the key takes to reach it — displayed as two paths:

Direct path — the exact sequence of questions and answers the ID key follows, step by step, to arrive at this species.

Simulation CD path — an alternative route showing how the key still reaches the same species when the hardest-to-see characters (such as upperside features and forewing underside spaces 1–3, often hidden in resting photos) are all answered “Cannot determine”.

Visual Guide

Illustrated wing diagrams that introduce the anatomy and terminology — wing spaces, spot numbering, the end-cell bar, and more — used throughout the key and the scoring questions.

Where to start

Open Feature Scoring while referring to your photo. Mark only the features you can clearly see — candidates are ranked in real time. Leave anything unclear blank; unanswered questions do not penalise any candidate.

  • Use the Visual Guide to get familiar with the features and keys first.
  • Use Species Search to get familiar with the various features on Nacaduba and see how they derive the final species.

Know these five terms before you score

Wing spaces Each area between two adjacent veins is a numbered space. Spots are named by the space they sit in. Wing space diagram → HW spots 5, 6, 7 The row of hindwing postdiscal spots counted outward from the tornal margin. Their alignment — especially spot 6 — is the single most-used diagnostic character. HW spots diagram → End-cell bar The bar that closes the hindwing discal cell at its outer end. Where spot 6 sits relative to this bar is asked repeatedly. Spot 6 & end-cell bar →  Spot 6 position guide → FW spot 4 Forewing postdiscal spot in space 4. In some groups it is shifted outward, breaking the straight postdiscal row. FW spots diagram → FW space 11 A narrow costal space near the forewing apex. Presence of a central spot there is diagnostic in several subgroups. FW space 11 diagram →

Photo tip: the forewing base is usually hidden under the hindwing in resting shots — skip any feature you cannot clearly see.
