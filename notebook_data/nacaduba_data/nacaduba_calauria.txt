Nacaduba calauria malayica, also known as the Malay 6-line Blue, belongs to the berenice group (the 6-line Blues) of the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: As a member of the 6-line Blues, the underside of the forewing possesses a pair of pale subbasal lines across the cell.

General Pattern: The markings are formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae arranged in the usual Lycaenine pattern on a buff-grey ground color.

Distinguishing Features:
It is very similar in size and appearance to Nacaduba berenice.

Unlike Nacaduba beroe, the wing bases on the underside are not darkened.

Unlike Nacaduba kurava, the underside markings are not visible by transparency on the upperside of the male.

The male is a deeper purple-blue than N. berenice and lacks the white ribbon scales found in that species.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
