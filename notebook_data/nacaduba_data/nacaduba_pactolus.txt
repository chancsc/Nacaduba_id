Nacaduba pactolus odon (The Large 4-line Blue) is a member of the pavana group (the 4-line Blues) within the genus Nacaduba.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristic: The underside of the forewing lacks a pair of pale subbasal lines in the cell.

Pattern Style: The markings on the underside are formed by pairs of short, dull whitish striae. 

Primary Diagnostic Feature: It is characterized by having underside stripes (striae) that are broader and more diffuse than in any other species within the genus, with the exception of the much rarer N. angusta.

Ground Color: The area between the postdiscal striae is unicolorous and of the ground color, which is typically a buff-grey.

Striae Color: The striae themselves are notably diffuse and yellowish.

Wing Length and Size

Forewing Length: The forewing typically measures between 14.5 mm and 16.0 mm.

Tail Information

Tail: This species possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing.
